Script

Characters: Sarah – Stressed out from her workload.
David – Her husband, well-meaning but it's almost as if he's using weaponized incompetence to get out of chores.
Setting: Evening. The kitchen is cluttered but functional. School papers, a laptop, and a half-prepared dinner are scattered across the counters. Distant sounds of a television in the other room. Light from the window catches the movement of a tree outside. (SARAH is on the stove, stirring something in a pan. Her phone buzzes. She glances at it but doesn’t check. DAVID enters, holding his phone and half-finished laundry.)
DAVID
Hey, do you want me to start the laundry, or—
SARAH
(speaking over him)
Can you just do dinner tonight? I’ve got a call in half an hour and I haven’t even—
DAVID
(interrupting, shrugging)
Yeah, I mean, suree. But… you’re just better at this stuff. You know, cooking, planning, all that.
(SARAH stops stirring. Sets the spoon down.)
SARAH
I’m better at it because I have to be. Not because I want to be!
DAVID
It’s not a criticism. I just mean you’re organized. I’d probably mess it up.
SARAH
Exactly. You don’t try because you assume I’ll fix it.
DAVID
Come on. That’s not fair. I help. I work too. I make more money doesn’t that count?
SARAH
(staring at him)
You make more because I gave up promotions, shifts, and chances so someone could be here. So someone could remember what the kids need. So someone could manage everything else.
DAVID
We both made choices.
SARAH
No. I made space so you didn’t have to.
(Beat. Silence hangs in the air. SARAH turns away, and pushes the pan off the burner.)
SARAH
Do you know what it feels like to be needed by everyone but supported by no one?
(A child’s voice yells from the other room. Something crashes. SARAH flinches, and closes her eyes.)
DAVID
You’re making this into something bigger—
SARAH
(biting back)
Because it is something bigger.
(She takes her keys from the counter. Moves toward the door.)
SARAH
I’m stepping outside. Figure out dinner.
(She exits toward the willow tree outside)
