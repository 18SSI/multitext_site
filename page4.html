Bend

A step outside, the sky gone slate, The air was thick, with so much weight.
Clouds hung low, the stars unsure,
The kind of night that feels like a blur.
The door clicked shut behind her heel,
The kind of sound you’re not meant to feel.
Still clutching keys, she did not speak,
The quiet wrapped around her cheek.
The willow swayed, not out of grace,
But shaped by years in the same place.
Its limbs hung low, its shadow wide,
A figure bent, but dignified.
The moon, behind a gauzy sheet,
Cast silver lines along her feet.
She didn’t pray. She didn’t move.
She let the moment simply prove.
No song, no flame, no grand escape,
Just stars above in shifting shape.
And in that hush, beneath that tree,
She wasn’t free
but she could be.
